# Functional State Pool

A repeatable check-in to track how you are functioning.

Part of [Dissociative System Witness](https://github.com/FrontSwitch/ds-witness) — a local, private assessment tracker.
DS-Witness handles sampling, tracking, and visualization for this assessment and other datasets.

You can also use the questions and framework on their own, however you prefer.

---

## What

I wanted an assessment I could run frequently (daily or weekly).
Existing tools felt vague or misaligned.
So I tried to build something that would help identify how I was functioning.

**Functional State (FS)** refers to a person's operating capacity.

Like a computer system monitor that shows CPU usage, memory, and active processes, FS tracks how the system is running.
It shifts focus away from pathology and who is fronting.
It asks how the system is performing.

It is the difference between asking "How do you feel?" (subjective/narrative) and "How am I responding?" (objective/mechanical).

**Goal** - A compact, system-level answer to "How am I doing?"

The output helps gauge stability: sustainable or approaching a breakdown.


## How it works

A pool of 36 questions is sampled per run.
Each run presents 12 questions.

Each question is tagged with:

### Mechanism: What is the friction?

**Load** — capacity overwhelmed. Energy, sensory, or cognitive resources are depleted or exceeded. The system is running at or past its limits.

**Resistance** — pushback or avoidance. The system protects autonomy by pushing back against demands, commitments, or engagement — even when that engagement would help.

**Interference** — signal corruption. Something disrupts the normal flow — noise, intrusion, or a loss of agency. The right signal isn't getting through.

### Domain: Where is it showing up?

**Doing** - executive tasks. How the system initiates, sustains, and prioritizes action.
**Feeling** - internal sensations. How the system processes cognitive load, sensory input, and emotional signal.
**Being** - baseline presence. How the system maintains a coherent sense of self across time.
**Relating** - external interactions. How the system manages contact with others and the environment.

---

## Format

Preamble: *In the past week...*

Scale: 0 = Absent · 1 = Rare · 2 = Sometimes · 3 = Often · 4 = Constant

### Structure

* 36 total questions
* 4 domains x 3 subclasses x 3 mechanisms
* Each subclass contains:
  * 1 Load
  * 1 Resistance
  * 1 Interference

### Sampling

* Pick 1 question per subclass, 12 questions per run
* Cycle through all 3 questions before reshuffling

## Questions

### Doing

#### Initiation

| Type | Question |
|------|----------|
| **Load** | Simple tasks felt unusually heavy to begin. |
| **Resistance** | I avoided starting even when I knew it was manageable. |
| **Interference** | I got stuck before starting a task or transitioning. |

#### Completion

| Type | Question |
|------|----------|
| **Load** | I ran out of energy before finishing something I started. |
| **Resistance** | I abandoned tasks when they became effortful. |
| **Interference** | I lost track of what I was doing mid-task. |

#### Priority

| Type | Question |
|------|----------|
| **Load** | I stopped seeing or ignored physical reminders I set for myself. |
| **Resistance** | I focused on low-priority tasks over important ones. |
| **Interference** | Important tasks kept slipping from awareness. |

### Feeling

#### Cognitive

| Type | Question |
|------|----------|
| **Load** | Thinking felt slow or effortful. |
| **Resistance** | I avoided engaging with difficult thoughts. |
| **Interference** | Internal chatter made it hard to think clearly. |

#### Sensory

| Type | Question |
|------|----------|
| **Load** | I had to shut down or withdraw due to overload. |
| **Resistance** | I avoided sensory input that felt like too much. |
| **Interference** | Sensory input felt distorted or distant. |

#### Emotional

| Type | Question |
|------|----------|
| **Load** | I could tell I should be having an emotion, but the signal didn't reach me. |
| **Resistance** | I pushed away feelings before they could register. |
| **Interference** | I was having emotions but didn't know to whom they belonged. |

### Being

#### Identity

| Type | Question |
|------|----------|
| **Load** | Maintaining a stable sense of self felt effortful. |
| **Resistance** | I avoided connecting to a sense of who I am. |
| **Interference** | I felt disconnected from my body or sense of self. |

#### Continuity

| Type | Question |
|------|----------|
| **Load** | Future plans felt irrelevant because that 'me' doesn't exist now |
| **Resistance** | I avoided thinking about upcoming responsibilities. |
| **Interference** | Today, yesterday, and next week felt like they were in the wrong order. |

#### Agency

| Type | Question |
|------|----------|
| **Load** | I felt like an observer rather than an agent. |
| **Resistance** | I resisted committing to decisions or directions. |
| **Interference** | I was unsure who was fronting or in charge. |

### Relating

#### Boundaries

| Type | Question |
|------|----------|
| **Load** | Small demands felt disproportionately intrusive. |
| **Resistance** | I felt crowded or defensive, even alone. |
| **Interference** | Others' needs overrode my own sense of priority. |

#### Autonomy

| Type | Question |
|------|----------|
| **Load** | I felt pressure against limits I couldn't define. |
| **Resistance** | I resisted structure even when it would help. |
| **Interference** | I felt like I was following rather than choosing. |

#### Reactivity

| Type | Question |
|------|----------|
| **Load** | A request felt like an irritation or a physical attack. |
| **Resistance** | I stayed up late to reclaim personal time. |
| **Interference** | My responses to others didn't feel like "mine." |
